Electrical Inspection & Testing in East Kilbride — Common Questions

How often does a rented property need an EICR in Scotland?
At least every 5 years, or at change of tenancy if the previous report is due to expire — it's a legal requirement under the Repairing Standard.
How long does testing take?
A typical flat or house takes 2–4 hours on site. Larger commercial properties take longer, which we'll scope in advance.
Do landlords in East Kilbride legally need an EICR?
Yes — landlords letting property in Scotland, including in East Kilbride, have a legal duty to have a satisfactory Electrical Installation Condition Report (EICR) in place, generally renewed every five years. We're happy to confirm exactly where your property stands.
How long does an inspection take for commercial and mixed-use units?
Commercial EICRs in East Kilbride vary with the size of the unit, but most are completed within a day, with a written report to follow.
What happens if the inspection finds a problem?
We'll explain exactly what's classified as needing attention and why, give you a clear quote for any remedial work in East Kilbride, and there's never any pressure to book it with us on the spot.
