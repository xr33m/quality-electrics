Behind The Job

Planning Your Kitchen's Electrics Before the Units Go In

Kitchens carry more electrical load than any other room in the house, so getting the plan right before first fix matters more here than almost anywhere else. Ovens, hobs, and induction hobs in particular need their own dedicated circuit sized to that specific appliance — not shared with your general sockets — which is something we agree with you and your kitchen fitter before any cabinets go in.

Socket and lighting positions need to be locked in at first fix too, ideally before the units are ordered, since moving a socket after the cabinets are fitted means undoing tiling and worktops. Under-cabinet and feature lighting is easiest to wire in cleanly at this stage as well, so there's no visible cabling once everything's installed.

FAQs

Common Questions About Kitchen Electrical Installations

Do I need a full re-wire of the kitchen, or just new circuits for the appliances?
Usually just dedicated circuits for the new appliances and any layout changes — a full kitchen rewire is only needed if the existing wiring itself is old or undersized. We'll tell you honestly which applies once we've seen it.
Will the power be off while you work?
Only for the circuits we're connecting into, agreed around your kitchen fitter's schedule so it doesn't hold up the wider job.
How long will it take?
First and second fix combined is typically 1–2 days, depending on the size of the kitchen and how much lighting is involved.
When should I book you in relative to my kitchen fitter?
Before the units go in, ideally at first-fix stage, so sockets and circuits land in the right places once cabinets are fitted.
Can you work directly with my kitchen designer or fitter?
Yes, we regularly work from kitchen design plans and coordinate directly with the fitter.
Do induction hobs need a dedicated circuit?
Yes, almost always — we size the circuit to the specific hob you're installing.
Do you supply the sockets and lighting, or do I?
Either — we can supply everything, or fit fixtures and fittings you've already chosen.
How long does a kitchen electrical fit take?
