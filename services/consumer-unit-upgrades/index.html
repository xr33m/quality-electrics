Behind The Job

RCDs, RCBOs, and Why Your Board Actually Matters

An old rewireable fuse box protects your wiring from overheating — that's it. It does nothing to protect a person from electric shock, because it has no RCD (residual current device) protection, which is why boards like these are one of the most common issues we flag on an EICR.

A modern consumer unit fits RCDs or RCBOs (a combined breaker and RCD per circuit) as standard, which cut the power in milliseconds if they detect current leaking somewhere it shouldn't — like through a person. We size and split circuits properly across the board so one nuisance trip doesn't take out your whole house, which is the other common complaint we hear about old or badly-specified boards.

FAQs

Common Questions About Consumer Unit Upgrades

Do I need a whole new board, or can mine be repaired?
If it's an old rewireable fuse box with no RCD protection, repairing it isn't really an option — it needs replacing to meet current safety standards. If it's a modern board with one faulty part, sometimes a repair genuinely is enough, and we'll tell you honestly which applies.
Is my current board safe to keep using in the meantime?
We'll tell you straight during the survey — if it's a serious risk we won't let you sit on it, but plenty of ageing boards are fine to use short-term while you plan the upgrade.
Will the power be off during the work?
Yes, temporarily — we agree a time that works for you and keep the power-down window as short as possible.
