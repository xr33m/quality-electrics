Behind The Job

What Actually Happens During an EV Charger Install

It starts with the free survey, not the fitting — we check your incoming supply, existing consumer unit, and the cable route between the board and where the charger's going, because that's what decides the real cost and timeline, not the charger itself.

On install day, we run the cable, mount the unit, and connect it into your consumer unit — upgrading the board first if the survey flagged it as necessary. Every charger is then load-tested, commissioned, and certified before we hand it over.

Since 2022, every new home and workplace charger in Great Britain is legally required to have smart charging capability, meaning it can be scheduled and monitored remotely — that's standard on everything we fit, not an optional extra.

FAQs

Common Questions About EV Charger Installation

Will my existing electrics actually cope with a charger?
That's exactly what the free survey is for — we check your supply and consumer unit before quoting, so there are no surprises once work starts.
How long will the power be off for?
Usually a short isolation while we connect into the consumer unit, not an all-day outage — we'll tell you the expected window before we start.
How long does the whole job take?
Most home installs are done within half a day, start to finish.
Do I need planning permission for a home charge point?
Almost never for a standard domestic installation. We'll flag it if your property is an exception, such as a listed building.
How long does installation take?
Most home installs are done within half a day, depending on cable run length and whether any consumer unit work is needed.
What if my consumer unit can't handle a charger?
We check this during the free survey. If an upgrade is needed, we'll quote for it alongside the charger install.
Are smart chargers a legal requirement?
Yes — since 2022, new home and workplace chargers in Great Britain must have smart charging capability by law. All our installs meet this.
Can you install chargers for businesses and fleets?
Yes, including multi-charger workplace setups with load balancing so you're not paying to upgrade your whole supply unnecessarily.
